ET 1982

What is ET 1982

ET 1982 is one of the worst games ever released. It near single handedly crashed the entire video game industry in the 1980s. It was Developed and published by Atari and was primarily developed by Howard Scott Warshaw. The game is a top down adventure game where you play as ET trying to avoid being caught by government agents

The Background

Following the success of the movie ET the CEO of Atari's parent company began negotiations with Steven Spielburg and Univeral Pictures to make a Game based on ET. Atari soon paid around 20 million dollars (~65 Million Today) in order to get the rights to produce the game. It was soon developed in 5 weeks by Howard Scott Warshaw for 200,000$ and a week long trip to Hawaii.

Public Responce

ET was highly anticipated and was believed to be the beginning of wave of games based on movies. Sadly things did not go this way. Instead ET was highly disliked with around 25% of copies bought in the intial wave being returned. The game was highly disliked by audiences including many children saying the game "sucked".
